# Prosthetic Outreach Initiative — Volunteer Coordination

Coordinates volunteer tasks across outreach teams for affordable prosthetic solutions.

## 📋 Task Categories

| # | Category | Description |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Outreach & Community Engagement** | Contact hospitals, clinics, and NGOs |
| 2 | **Logistics & Supply Chain** | Coordinate prosthetic component sourcing and shipment |
| 3 | **Clinical Research Support** | Assist with trial data collection and documentation |
| 4 | **Training & Education** | Develop training materials for prosthetic fitting and use |
| 5 | **Fundraising & Grants** | Identify funding opportunities and draft proposals |
| 6 | **Data & Analytics** | Compile trial results, track outcomes, generate reports |

## 🔑 Key Clinical Trials

### Affordable Prosthetics Focus

| Trial | NCT ID | Focus | Status |
|-------|--------|-------|--------|
| ReMotion Knee — Reverse Innovation | [NCT04700085](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04700085) | $80 affordable prosthetic knee (vs. $1,000–$4,000 standard) | Completed |
| Motor Compensation Post-Amputation | [NCT06780943](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06780943) | Locomotor adaptations with prosthetics; biomechanics & proprioception | Recruiting |
| Prosthesis Training & Phantom Exercises | [NCT06013631](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06013631) | Training effects on satisfaction and ambulation | Completed |
| PlayBionic: Mobile Training App | [NCT03547310](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03547310) | Game-based prosthesis control training | Unknown |

### Trial Highlights

- **ReMotion Knee** ($80): A 3-5 year lifespan mechanical knee developed by D-Rev; ISO 10328 certified, CE Mark approved. Henry Ford Health System led U.S. study comparing it against standard $1,000–$4,000 knees.
- **QAMPA**: 75-participant study at CHU de Nîmes characterizing post-amputation locomotor adaptations.
- **PlayBionic**: Myoelectric prosthesis training via smartphone game (MyoBeatz) for upper-extremity amputees.
